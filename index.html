The Market Gap

Most companies understand
the potential of RGM.

Many organizations understand that better pricing decisions, optimized promotions, and disciplined assortment strategies can significantly improve both revenue growth and margins. Yet implementing RGM successfully is often difficult — most companies face two imperfect alternatives.

Traditional Consultants

Consulting firms provide strategic recommendations and analytical frameworks. However, once the project ends, internal teams are often left without the expertise to interpret the analysis and execute the recommendations.

The strategy exists — but the execution does not.

Strategy without execution

Technology Platforms

Advanced RGM platforms provide powerful tools for modeling pricing and promotions. But these platforms require experienced teams capable of interpreting data, building models, and translating insights into commercial decisions.

Without this expertise, the technology often remains underutilized.

Revenue Growth Levers

The Four Revenue Growth Levers

The primary commercial mechanisms through which RGM creates measurable business impact.

01

Pricing

Defining price architecture, guardrails, and optimization models that maximize revenue without sacrificing volume or competitive position.

02

Promotions

Designing promotional strategies that drive truly incremental sales — not just volume that would have occurred regardless of the investment.

03

Assortment

Optimizing product portfolios to improve mix, availability, and profitability across categories, channels, and customer segments.

04

Trade Investment

Managing trade spending to maximize return on investment and ensure every dollar of trade funds is aligned with commercial strategy.
